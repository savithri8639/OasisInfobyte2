History of Taj Mahal

Symbol of Love❤️

Commissioned by Shah Jahan

The Taj Mahal is an iconic monument located in Agra, India. It was commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away during childbirth in 1631. Shah Jahan was deeply grief-stricken by her death and wanted to create a grand mausoleum to honor her memory.Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 under the supervision of the chief architect, Ustad Ahmad Lahori, and a team of skilled craftsmen and artisans. The construction of the main mausoleum took approximately 16 years to complete, with additional structures and the surrounding gardens finished later.Often referred to as the "Symbol of Love," the Taj Mahal is a testament to the deep love and affection Shah Jahan had for Mumtaz Mahal.The monument's design and symmetry are said to reflect the affection Shah Jahan had for Mumtaz

Mahal.The monument's design and symmetry are said to reflect the perfection of their love.

Architectural Marvel

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning architectural beauty and intricate craftsmanship. It combines elements of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The mausoleum is primarily made of white marble with exquisite inlay work using semi-precious stones like lapis lazuli, turquoise, and others. Today, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors from around the world who come to admire its beauty, learn about its history, and pay their respects to the enduring love story it represents. It remains a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage and a masterpiece of world architecture.In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance. It has

also been featured on the list of the "New Seven Wonders of the World."

The Architecture Of Taj Mahal

Celestial Beauty

The Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, renowned for its unparalleled beauty and symmetry. Constructed in the 17th century in Agra, India, it is primarily made of gleaming white marble, quarried from distant Rajasthan. At its heart stands a grand mausoleum with a majestic central dome flanked by four elegant minarets. The intricate inlay work using semi-precious stones adorns the marble surfaces with delicate floral and geometric patterns, while verses from the Quran grace the edifice. Surrounding the mausoleum are meticulously landscaped gardens divided by water channels and a reflective pool, offering a sense of paradise on Earth. The Taj Mahal's architectural perfection lies in its perfect symmetry, the mesmerizing play of light on its marble surfaces throughout the day, and its exquisite craftsmanship, making it an enduring symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
